Cryptocurrency Exchanges Explained

What is a Crypto Exchange?

An exchange is a platform where you can buy, sell, and trade cryptocurrencies. Exchanges act as intermediaries between buyers and sellers, providing liquidity and market access.

üè¢ Centralized Exchanges (CEX)

Examples: Coinbase, Binance, Kraken

Advantages:

  • High liquidity & trading volume
  • User-friendly interfaces
  • Fiat currency support (USD, EUR)
  • Customer support services
  • Advanced trading features

Disadvantages:

  • Requires KYC verification
  • Exchange controls your funds
  • Hack risk (Mt. Gox, FTX)
  • Trading fees (0.1% - 4%)
  • Regulatory restrictions

Best for: Beginners, high-volume traders

üåê Decentralized Exchanges (DEX)

Examples: Uniswap, PancakeSwap, dYdX

Advantages:

  • No KYC required
  • You control your funds
  • Lower fees on some networks
  • Access to new tokens early
  • Resistant to censorship

Disadvantages:

  • Steeper learning curve
  • Lower liquidity for some pairs
  • No customer support
  • Smart contract risks
  • Only crypto-to-crypto trading

Best for: Experienced users, privacy seekers
